Museum Highlights

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

11:30 A.M.–12:30 P.M.

Discover The Met collection on this tour, which focuses on a selection of works of art representing different cultures and time periods and encourages visitors to explore the Museum on their own.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall

Great Sculpture: Ancient to Contemporary

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

11:45 A.M.–12:45 P.M.

Explore sculptural masterworks dating from ancient times to the present. Three-dimensional objects from Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas offer multiple viewpoints on artistry and function.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall

A Cradle of Civilization: Art of the Ancient Near East

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

12:00–1:00 P.M.

Discover the extraordinary artistic achievements of the societies that thrived within a vast area stretching from the eastern Mediterranean to Central Asia and the Indus Valley. Pieces that include some of the world's earliest writing and a remarkable group of stone reliefs.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall

Arts of China

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

12:15–1:15 P.M.

This tour introduces visitors to five thousand years of Chinese art, including the arts of ancient China, the thousand-year-old tradition of painting and calligraphy, and the Astor Court, a seventeenth-century-style Ming garden created especially for the Metropolitan Museum.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall

Impressionism and Post-Impressionism

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

12:30–1:30 P.M.

The tour introduces visitors to the origins of Impressionism in the late 1860s and its flourishing in the following decades in landscapes and scenes from modern life by such artists as Monet and Renoir. Exploring also the impact, on the next generation of artists, which includes Cézanne and Van Gogh.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall

European Paintings: Renaissance to Revolution

WEDNESDAY / OCTOBER 3

1:45 P.M.–5:30 P.M.

The Met's world-famed collection of European paintings encompasses works of art from the 13th through the 19th centuries—from Giotto to Gauguin. Most, though not all, are displayed in the galleries of the Department of European Paintings.

At The Met Fifth Avenue - Tours Sign, Great Hall
